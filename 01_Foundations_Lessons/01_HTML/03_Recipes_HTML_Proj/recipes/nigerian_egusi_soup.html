Egusi Soup

Description

Egusi soup is a rich and flavorful West African dish made primarily from ground melon seeds (egusi), which give it a thick and hearty texture. It is commonly cooked with leafy vegetables such as spinach, pumpkin leaves, or bitter leaf, and features a blend of spices, palm oil, and proteins like beef, goat meat, fish, or crayfish. It is a staple in Nigerian cuisine and widely enjoyed across the region.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups egusi (melon seeds), ground
  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 1 lb (about 500g) assorted meat (beef, goat meat, tripe, etc.)
  • 1 cup stockfish (optional)
  • 1 cup smoked or dried fish, deboned
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2-3 cups vegetable (pumpkin leaves, spinach, or bitter leaf)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons locust beans (iru) (optional)
  • 4 cups meat stock (or water)
  • 3-4 tablespoons ground pepper (or to taste)
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. If using whole egusi seeds, grind them into a fine powder using a dry mill or blender.
  2. Wash and season the assorted meat with salt, seasoning cubes, onions, and some water. Boil until tender, then set aside. Soak the stockfish in warm water to soften it.
  3. Rinse and chop the vegetables of your choice (pumpkin leaves, spinach, or bitter leaf). If using bitter leaf, wash thoroughly to reduce bitterness.
  4. In a pot, heat the palm oil over medium heat. Do not overheat.
  5. Add the chopped onions and locust beans (iru) if using, and saut√© for about a minute.
  6. Mix the ground egusi with a little water to form a thick paste. Add this paste into the oil in small lumps and fry for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. This step enhances the flavor.
  7. Gradually pour in the meat stock (or water), stirring continuously to loosen the egusi. Let it cook for another 10 minutes.
  8. Add the cooked meat, stockfish, and smoked fish, then mix well. Allow it to simmer for another 5 minutes.
  9. Stir in ground crayfish, pepper, seasoning cubes, and salt to taste. Adjust the seasoning as needed.
  10. Finally, add the chopped vegetables and stir. Let it cook for another 3-5 minutes to preserve the color and nutrients.
